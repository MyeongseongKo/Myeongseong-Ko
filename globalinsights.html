Why Global Insights?

Recent developments in geopolitics, security, and environmental issues have profoundly impacted global economic dynamics. From the perspective of international trade, these factors can significantly reduce welfare in the long runâ€”especially if they lead to a rise in protectionism.

Caliendo and Parro (2015, Review of Economic Studies) quantitatively evaluate how NAFTA affected trade and welfare using a multisector Ricardian model with input-output linkages. Their findings suggest that trade barriers within GVCs amplify welfare effects across countries.

Similarly, Dekle, Eaton, and Kortum (2008, IMF Staff Papers) demonstrate that correcting trade imbalances through artificial policy instruments can reduce long-run welfare, even if trade remains balanced. These policies distort comparative advantage and reduce real consumption.

This page aims to provide rigorous, data-driven insights on such issues. As a researcher in international trade, I hope these analyses can contribute to global understanding, peace, and prosperity.

Geopolitical Risks, FDI, and GVC Fragmentation (2025)

Trade policy uncertainty and geopolitical tensions are on the rise globally. Prior studies show such uncertainty affects firm decisions significantly.

Julio and Yook (2016) and Helpman et al. (2004) confirm that FDI and exports are discouraged by political instability. The stability of GVCs is increasingly challenged.

Thus, international cooperation through trade agreements is even more critical than what traditional models predicted.
