Global Insights: Focus on FDI

Geopolitical Risks, FDI, and GVC Fragmentation (2025)

Recent studies and reports converge on one conclusion: heightened geopolitical tensions have substantially raised uncertainty around global value chains (GVCs) and deterred foreign direct investment (FDI) worldwide. Alessandria et al. (2022) document that trade policy volatility reduces firms’ cross-border activity. Tamberi (2024) emphasizes that policy-induced uncertainty systematically delays FDI decisions. Helpman, Melitz, and Yeaple (2004) highlight firms' threshold behavior under market entry uncertainty, exacerbated today by political shocks.

My Comment: Growing geopolitical tensions—recently noted by BBC and Reuters reports—are no longer episodic but structural. They now fundamentally reshape FDI flows, weakening GVC stability.
